Learn from building architects

Jun 04, 2024 in KOSTOF
architecture spiro kostof frank lloyd wright wright god karen moyer convenience henry wotton henry watton john ruskin architect ruskin lloyd
2 min read

Architecture is a social act and a material theater of human activity.Spiro Kostof (Spiro Kostof)

How many software architects consider their role to be exclusively (or primarily) technical?Shouldn’t they actually be intermediaries and arbitrators for the warring factions among the parties interested in the project? How many of them view their work purely from an intellectual standpoint, without giving due attention to the human factor?

An architect becomes great not so much through intellect as through a pure, sensitive heart.Frank Lloyd Wright (Frank Lloyd Wright)

What is more characteristic of the architects in your organization: unbridled intellectual power and the ability to remember the smallest technical details, or good taste, sophistication, and generosity of spirit?In what kind of atmosphere would you prefer to work yourself?

A doctor can bury his mistake, while an architect can only cover the walls with ivy.Frank Lloyd Wright

Isn’t “supporting inherited systems” just a trimming of this ivy?Do you, as an architect, have the strength of spirit to destroy your own unsuccessful creation?Or would you prefer to just cover it up?Wright also said that the architect’s best friend is a sledgehammer.What have you broken with her lately?

Architects not only believe that they sit at God’s right hand, but also think that if God were to stand up, they would take His place.Karen Moyer (Karen Moyer)

Replace “God” with “the client.”

In architecture, as in all other applied arts, the ultimate goal guides the action. The ultimate goal is a well-constructed building. Such a building possesses three qualities: Convenience, Durability, and Aesthetics.Henry Wotton (Henry Watton)

When was the last time you came across a software product whose architecture brought you aesthetic pleasure?Do you yourself strive for your works to be aesthetic?

A person who is not a great sculptor or artist cannot be an architect. If he is not a sculptor, not an artist, then he can only be a builder.John Ruskin (John Ruskin)

Does aesthetics hold a worthy place in your architecture?Are you driven by an artistic interest in forms and textures, a sculptural sense of balance, a desire to convey movement, or a feeling of the importance of negative space when uniting components in the construction of systems?

And finally, a statement that requires no comments – a true remedy for the most destructive syndrome in the work of an architect:

This looks like a fantastic paradox, but nonetheless it is a crucial truth: absolutely perfect architecture cannot truly be great.John Ruskin

Success is hampered by scale Perhaps technology is not your primary issue
